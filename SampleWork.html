Reflection 1

In my research paper I explore the evolving security measures at Granite Bay High School and their impact on students, staff, and the learning environment. By analyzing policies such as security cameras, random drug searches, and enhanced locking mechanisms, the piece raises important questions about balancing safety and privacy. Through interviews, personal experiences, and critical analysis, it highlights the unintended consequences of these measures on student culture while advocating for mutual respect and transparent communication between faculty and students. This work reflects my belief in leading with purpose by emphasizing the need for thoughtful, student-centered policies that genuinely enhance safety without compromising trust. It also embodies inspiring through action by encouraging collaboration, open dialogue, and shared responsibility for creating a positive and secure educational community.

Reflection 3

In my research proposal, I examined the role of augmentative and alternative communication (AAC) devices in supporting individuals with speech impairments. While AAC devices can significantly improve communication for those with speech challenges, I also recognized that they are not the only solution. Other forms of communication, such as non-verbal cues, sign language, and gestures, can play equally important roles in enhancing understanding and connection. My research proposal sought to explore how a combination of these communication methods can be most effective, ensuring that individuals have access to the tools that best meet their needs. By emphasizing the importance of diverse communication strategies, my research proposal encourages a thoughtful and inclusive approach to empowering individuals with speech impairments to communicate effectively and confidently.
